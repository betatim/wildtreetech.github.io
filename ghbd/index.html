Geneva's Humanitarian Big Data

An introduction to the power of big data analysis for your humanitarian project.

Location: Impact Hub Geneva at 4pm on June 27th 2016

What can machine-learning do for your project?

Does your project have data in the form of prose? Faced with a large number of documents to process manually? Are you trying to model a real world process? Why not use your historical records to learn something new about your project, or even make predictions for the future!

Would your project require five PhD students and years of research time or are there off-the-shelf solutions? What can a group of tech savy people combined with domain experts achieve in a short amount of time? Attend this event and learn about the state of the art of big data analysis. Sign up here.

Join THE Port and Wild Tree Tech for this training event in Geneva. We will help you answer questions like:

  • Do I have big, medium, or small data?
  • What is machine-learning?
  • How hard is natural language processing really?
  • Is image recognition for me?
  • Modelling the real world without a model!??
  • What are others in this space doing?
  • How can I try all this without wasting my time?

Details

The event will take place at the Impact Hub Geneva at 4pm on June 27th 2016. There will be a presentation and demos by Tim Head followed by time for questions and answers.

Signup

To keep the event interactive, friendly and relevant spaces are limited to 20 attendees (first come, first served). Registration fee: CHF40. Sign up here.

Chatham House Rule

To further frank questions and discussion the event is held under the Chatham House Rule: participants are free to use the information received, but neither the identity nor the affiliation of the speaker(s), nor that of any other participant, may be revealed.
